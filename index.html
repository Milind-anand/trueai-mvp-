Our Science

How Verai detects AI content

Verai uses a multi-signal detection approach powered by Gemini AI — analyzing the same linguistic patterns that distinguish AI writing from human writing, across text, web pages and videos.

📊
Perplexity Analysis
AI models always pick the statistically most likely next word. This makes AI text highly predictable. Verai measures how "surprising" each word choice is — low perplexity means AI, high perplexity means human. This is the same core signal used by GPTZero and Originality.ai.
📈
Burstiness Score
Humans write in bursts — mixing short punchy sentences with long complex ones. AI writes uniformly — every sentence tends to be similar in length and complexity. Verai measures this variation (burstiness). Low burstiness is a strong AI signal.
🔤
AI Vocabulary Fingerprint
AI models overuse specific words — "furthermore", "leverage", "delve", "multifaceted", "paramount", "transformative", "comprehensive", "it is important to note". Verai has a trained dictionary of 200+ AI-typical phrases and scores their frequency in the text.
🧠
Structural Pattern Detection
AI writes in perfect paragraph blocks — always 3-5 sentences, always balanced, never tangents, never repetition. Humans ramble, repeat themselves differently, go off-topic, and have uneven structure. Verai detects this structural uniformity as an AI signal.
🫂
Human Authenticity Markers
Real human writing contains typos, self-corrections, colloquialisms, hedging language ("I think", "maybe"), personal anecdotes, and specific details only a human would know. Verai actively looks for these markers and treats their presence as a strong human signal.
🔍
Sentence-Level Classification
Verai doesn't just give a single score for the whole document — it classifies each sentence individually and highlights the specific sentences most likely written by AI. This lets you see exactly which parts of a document are suspicious.
